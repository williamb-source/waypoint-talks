We've all been there. Staring at a decision that feels impossible. Weighing options that all seem equally valid — or equally terrifying. The more we think, the more confused we get.

Sound familiar?

## The Paradox of Overthinking

Here's what I've learned from working with clients: **the problem is rarely a lack of information**. It's usually the opposite. We have too much information, too many opinions, too many "what ifs" swirling around in our heads.

We think that if we just analyze the situation a little more, the right answer will reveal itself. But it rarely works that way. Instead, we end up in analysis paralysis — stuck in an endless loop of thinking without moving.

## What Actually Creates Clarity

Clarity doesn't come from more thinking. It comes from **creating space to hear yourself think**.

That's a subtle but crucial difference.

When you're constantly consuming information — reading articles, asking for advice, scrolling through other people's success stories — you're filling up all the space in your mind. There's no room left for your own wisdom to emerge.

Real clarity happens when you:

1. **Slow down** — Stop the frantic search for the "right" answer
2. **Get quiet** — Create space away from other people's opinions
3. **Listen** — Pay attention to what you already know but haven't acknowledged

## A Simple Exercise

Next time you're stuck on a decision, try this:

Set a timer for 10 minutes. Sit somewhere quiet with a blank piece of paper. At the top, write the decision you're facing. Then just... write. Stream of consciousness. No editing, no judgment.

Don't try to solve anything. Just let your thoughts flow onto the page.

When the timer goes off, read what you wrote. Often, buried somewhere in that mess of words, you'll find a hint of what you actually want. A sentence that makes you pause. A fear you've been avoiding. A truth you've been dancing around.

That's the beginning of clarity.

## You Don't Need All the Answers

Here's the thing I tell every client: **you don't need to figure out your whole life**. You just need to know your next step.

One clear step forward is infinitely more valuable than a hundred hypothetical plans. Because once you take that step, you'll see the next one. And the next. The path reveals itself as you walk it.

So stop trying to see the whole journey. Just find the next step.
